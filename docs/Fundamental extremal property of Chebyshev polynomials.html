Fundamental extremal property of Chebyshev polynomials

Chebyshev Interpolation Numeric Methods Theorem

1 Fundamental extremal property of Chebyshev polynomials

Let \(n \geq 1\)

\(\forall{}P(x) = 2^{n-1}x^n + \cdots\) is true \(\max_{x\in[-1,1]}\limits{}(\lvert{}P(x)\rvert{})\geq\max_{x\in[-1,1]}\limits{}(\lvert{}T_{n}(x)\rvert{}) = 1\)

The equality is reached \(\Leftrightarrow P(x) \equiv T_{n}(x)\)

1.1 Proof

We will prove that if \(P(x) = 2^{n-1}x^n + \cdots : \max_{x \in [-1, 1]}\limits{}\lvert{}P(x)\rvert{} \leq 1\) then \(P(x) = T_{n}(x)\)

Let \(Q(x) = T_n(x) - P(x)\)

\(Q \in \pi_{n-1}\)

We will prove that \(Q\) has \(n\) zeroes in \([-1, 1]\) from where \(Q \equiv 0 \Rightarrow P \equiv T_{n}\)

Let \(\eta_k, \quad k = \overline{1, n}\) be the extreme points of a Chebyshev polynomial.

Then \(Q(\eta_k) = T_{n}(\eta_{k}) - P(\eta_{k}) = (-1)^k - P(\eta_k)\) \(\lvert{}P(\eta_k)\rvert{} \leq 1 \Rightarrow \text{sign}Q(\eta_k) = (-1)^{k} or Q(\eta_k) = 0\)
