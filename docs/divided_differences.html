1 Divided Differences

Let \(x_0,\dots,x_n\) be different points Let \(f\) be defined for \(x_0,\dots,x_n\)

The divided difference of \(f\) in \(x_0,...,x_n\) is \(f[x_0,\dots,x_n]\)

It's recurrently defined as:

\begin{align*} f[x_0,\dots,x_n] &= \frac{f[x_1,\dots,x_n] - f[x_0,\dots,x_{n-1}]}{x_n-x_0} \tag{$n \geq 1$} \\ f[x_i] &= f(x_{i}) \tag{$i = \overline{0, n}$} \\ \end{align*}
